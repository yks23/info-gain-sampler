Improving Sampling for Masked Diffusion Models via Information Gain

Kaisen Yang, Jayden Teoh, Kaicheng Yang, Yitong Zhang, Alex Lamb

Tsinghua University · Singapore Management University · Shanghai Jiao Tong University · Beihang University

Paper Code
Abstract Method Results BibTeX

Abstract

Masked Diffusion Models (MDMs) offer greater flexibility in decoding order than autoregressive models but require careful planning to achieve high-quality generation. Existing samplers typically adopt greedy heuristics and ignore how current decoding choices affect future steps. We propose Info-Gain Sampler, a training-free decoding framework that balances immediate uncertainty with information gain over remaining masked tokens. Across reasoning, coding, creative writing, and image generation, Info-Gain Sampler consistently outperforms prior greedy baselines.

Method

Overview of Info-Gain Sampler: it evaluates candidate decoding actions by combining immediate confidence and expected reduction of future uncertainty.

Key Results

  • +3.6% average accuracy improvement on 6 reasoning benchmarks (full-attention MDM).
  • 63.1% average win-rate in creative writing evaluation.
  • Reduces cumulative uncertainty from 78.4 to 48.6 on reasoning tasks.
  • +1.9% gain on GenEval for text-to-image generation.
